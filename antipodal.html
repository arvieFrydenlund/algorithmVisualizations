A line of support to a set is a line L that touches the convex hull and all points lie on or to one side of L. Two points a and b are antipodal if they admit parallel lines of support. This is a Rotating Calipers Algorithm. 
            
    Input: A set P of n points in the plain
    Output: A list of pairs of antipodal points.
                    
    1.  Find the convex hull of P, call it CH
    2.  Find the points of CH with the lowest and highest points, let them be p1 and p2
    3.  Let L1 and L2 be the support lines for p1 and p2
    4.  Let the slope of L1 and L2 be 0
    5.  do
    6.     if the angle between line p1p1.next() and L1 > line p2p2.next() and L2
    7.        slope = slope of p2p2.next()
    8.        p2 = p2.next()
    9.        Pairs.add(p1, p2)
    10.    else if the angle between line p1p1.next() and L1 < line p2p2.next() and L2
    11.       slope = slope of p1p1.next()
    12.       p1 = p1.next()
    13.       Pairs.add(p1, p2)
    14.    else                                 //special case: parallel lines
    15.       slope = slope of p1p1.next()
    16.       Pairs.add(p1, p2.next())
    17.       Pairs.add(p1.next(), p2)
    18.       p1 = p1.next()
    19.       p2 = p2.next()
    20.       Pairs.add(p1, p2)
    21. while(p1 and p2 are different from the starting pair)
The running time is O(nlogn).
