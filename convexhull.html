A convex hull of a set of points P, CH(P), is the intersection of all convex sets containing P. 
            
    Input: A set P of n points in the plain
    Output: A list of vertices of CH(P) in clockwise order
                    
    1.  Sort P by x-coord using a lexographic ordering
    2.  Put p1 then p2 into a list upperHull
    3.  for i = 3 to n
    4.     Push pi to upperHull
    5.     while upperHull contains more than two points and 
           the last three points in upperHull do not make a right hand turn
    6.        Delete the middle of the three points from upperHull
                
    7.  Put pn then pn-1 into a list lowerHull
    8.  for i = n-2 to 1
    9.     Push pi to lowerHull
    10.    while lowerHull contains more than two points and 
           the last three points in lowerHull do not make a right hand turn
    11.       Delete the middle of the three points from lowerHull
                
    12. Remove the first and last points of lowerHull
    13. Return a list of the lowerHull appended to upperHull
                
    Where we can determine a right hand turn by taking the cross product of
    the vectors formed from the three points as in the following function:
                
    Input:  Three points in a given order
    Output:  > 0 if they do not make a right hand turn, 0 if they are co-linear,
             and < 0 if they do make a right hand turn.
                
    1.  notRightHandTurn(p1, p2, p3)
    2.     Return (p2.x - p1.x)*(p3.y - p1.y) - (p2.y - p1.y)*(p3.x - p1.x)
The running time is O(nlogn).

Algorithm Source - Computational Geometry: Algorithms and Applications, Third Edition, Mark de Berg et al.
